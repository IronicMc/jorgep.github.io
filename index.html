What does it mean to be college and career ready?

Being college and career ready means that a person possesses the skills, knowledge, and credentials needed to successfully transition from high school to post-secondary education or directly into the workforce. This includes having a strong academic foundation, effective communication and problem-solving skills, and the ability to adapt to new challenges and opportunities. Additionally, being college and career ready often involves obtaining relevant certifications or degrees, developing a professional network, and setting clear goals for future success.

What can i get with differet levels of education?

different levels of education can lead to different job opportunities. Generally, higher levels of education, such as a college degree or advanced degree, can qualify you for higher-paying and more advanced positions. However, there are also many jobs that do not require a formal education and instead value skills and experience. It is important to consider the specific industry and job market when deciding on the level of education needed for a particular job.

Highschool diploma

Individuals with a high school diploma can access a variety of job opportunities in fields such as administrative work, customer service, retail, security, construction, transportation, healthcare, banking, hairstyling, and food service. While these roles typically do not demand a college degree, advancement is possible through commitment, effort, and potentially acquiring further training or certifications.

2 Year College

Individuals with a 2-year college degree, or associate degree, have access to a wide range of job opportunities in various industries. Examples of jobs that typically require an associate degree include administrative assistant, dental assistant, electrician, graphic designer, HVAC technician, legal assistant, medical assistant, police officer, paralegal, and radiologic technologist. Many other career paths are also available to those with an associate degree, and individuals may be eligible for higher-paying roles, career advancement, and potential transfer to a 4-year college for a bachelor's degree in the future.

4 Year University

Individuals with a bachelor's degree have diverse job opportunities in various industries such as accounting, engineering, healthcare, education, and technology. Possessing this degree allows individuals to pursue roles like accountant, business manager, civil engineer, nurse, software developer, educator, social worker, and public relations specialist. Depending on the field of study, individuals can access higher-level positions, career advancement, and increased earning potential.
